# E-Commerce SQL Analysis Report

> A comprehensive analysis of 1,100 customer transactions covering sales performance, product profitability, acquisition channels, and payment behavior.

---

## Executive Summary

This analysis examined **1,100 e-commerce transactions** to uncover actionable business insights. Key findings at a glance:

- **Top acquisition channel:** Facebook (227 orders)
- **Most preferred payment method:** Online Wallet (248 orders)
- **Top revenue-generating products:** Chair, Laptop, Printer, Tablet, Monitor
- All major product categories exceeded **$5,000 in revenue**, indicating a healthy, balanced portfolio
- High-value orders (>$500) contribute disproportionately to total revenue

---

## 1. Dataset Overview

The dataset contains **1,100 customer orders** — a sufficiently large sample to analyze sales performance, customer purchasing behavior, payment preferences, and marketing effectiveness.

---

## 2. Order Fulfillment

A significant portion of orders reached **Delivered** status, indicating that the fulfillment process is functioning effectively. Delivered orders serve as a reliable representation of completed sales and actual customer purchasing patterns.

---

## 3. Revenue Performance

### Total & Average Revenue

- The business generated **substantial cumulative revenue** across all recorded transactions — the primary indicator of overall sales performance.
- The **Average Order Value (AOV)** reflects typical spending per transaction and benchmarks the effectiveness of pricing, bundling, and promotional strategies.

### High-Value Orders

Orders exceeding **$500** are present throughout the dataset, confirming the existence of a premium customer segment. These transactions contribute disproportionately to total revenue and warrant close monitoring.

---

### Top 5 Products by Revenue

All major product categories surpassed the **$5,000 revenue threshold**, confirming that no product is severely underperforming. The portfolio is healthy and well-balanced.

Average unit prices vary significantly across products, reflecting a diverse portfolio of both **budget and premium items**. Higher-priced products generate more revenue per transaction, while lower-priced products rely on volume.

## 5. Customer Acquisition Channels

| Source | Orders |
|--------|--------|
| Facebook | **227** ✦ Highest |
| Instagram | 223 |
| Referral | 222 |
| Email | 217 |
| Google | 211 |

**Key Insight:** Acquisition is broadly balanced across all channels. However, **social media channels (Facebook, Instagram) outperform search-based acquisition (Google)** in driving conversions. Facebook leads with the highest order count, making it the most effective channel for paid and organic campaigns.

---

## 6. Payment Method Preferences

| Payment Method | Orders |
|----------------|--------|
| Online Wallet | **248** ✦ Most popular |
| Cash | 246 |
| Credit Card | 235 |
| Debit Card | 222 |
| Gift Card | 149 |

**Key Insight:** Customers show strong preference for both digital and traditional payment methods. **Online Wallet leads**, while **Cash remains highly popular** despite increasing digital adoption. Gift Cards are the least utilized and represent an opportunity for promotional campaigns to increase adoption.

---

## 7. Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following actions are recommended:

1. **Double down on Facebook and Instagram** — these channels drive the most conversions and offer the highest ROI for marketing spend.
2. **Prioritize top products (Chair, Laptop, Printer)** in inventory and promotions — they are the strongest revenue contributors.
3. **Optimize Online Wallet and Cash checkout experiences** to reduce friction for the majority of customers.
4. **Launch Gift Card promotions** to boost adoption of the least-used payment method.
5. **Monitor high-value orders (>$500)** as a dedicated segment for retention and loyalty programs.

*Analysis based on 1,100 transactions from the cleaned e-commerce dataset.*
